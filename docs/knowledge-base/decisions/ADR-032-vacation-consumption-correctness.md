## Context

ADR-031 (S63) made the vacation **day-count** flat (25/5 regardless of part-time) and bound a "§6 stk.2 consumption conversion" (`5 ÷ work_days_per_week` feriedage per day off, requiring a new dated field) as launch-blocking. S66 Step-0 research **refuted that premise against primary sources** (see the research doc): §6 stk.2 is a week-mirroring/placement rule, the state Ferievejledning contains no 5÷N multiplier anywhere, and særlige-feriedag consumption is explicitly `hours-off ÷ that-day's scheduled hours` (Example 3.5) — one whole workday off = 1 dag regardless of days/week. Under the system's shape-free norm model (norm spread over 5 weekdays) the §6 folding is self-enforcing for week blocks; a 5÷N multiplier would over-consume.

What the refinement's code-level verification DID establish as the real defect set:

1. **The day-equivalent basis is ambiguous today.** Consumption divides recorded absence hours by flat 7.4 (`EntitlementMapping.StandardDayHours`), the per-day absence guard caps at flat 7.4, and the omitted-hours default is flat 7.4 — while the per-day **norm** shown in the same Skema grid is fraction-scaled (`weekly × fraction ÷ 5`) and the FE requires hand-typed hours with no default. A half-timer typing their natural full day (3.7) consumes 0.5 feriedag (⇒ 10 weeks of vacation; the law grants everyone 5 weeks); typing 7.4 instead over-credits their payroll norm. Whichever they type, one side is wrong. Live-representable today ⇒ launch-relevant.
2. **Consumption is recorded in two unreconciled shapes.** The booking path persists counters (`entitlement_balances.used`) while year-overview `afholdt`/`saldo` re-derive from absence rows — and `transferable` + tiles ride the persisted counters **in the same response**. Once the formula depends on dated profile fields and live-resolved config (`ConfigResolutionService.ResolveAsync` has no date parameter), the shapes diverge.
3. **An open bypass exists.** `POST /api/absences` (`TimeEndpoints.cs:163-247`) writes `AbsenceRegistered` events with no quota validation, no balance adjustment, no per-day guard; its FE consumers are router-orphaned S9 remnants, but the endpoint is live employee-self-callable surface.

Owner ratifications 2026-06-07: norm-based basis (OQ-1a); per-absence recorded valuation (OQ-3i); **drop the 5÷N engine + `work_days_per_week` field; ADR-031 D6 gate satisfied by this ADR's D1–D3** (premise re-adjudication after adversarial verification).

## Decision

**D1 — Canonical day basis: one day off = the employee's dated per-day system norm.** For an absence on date *d* by employee *e*: `dayEquivalent = hours ÷ fullDayHours(e, d)` where `fullDayHours` is the S65 `DailyNormCalculator` per-day value (dated profile fraction + dated agreement/OK anchoring). This is **exactly the mechanism the state authority prescribes** (Ferievejledning Example 3.5: fraction = hours-off ÷ that-day's scheduled hours). Applies to ALL entitlement-consuming absence types — VACATION, SPECIAL_HOLIDAY (the verified flat treatment — no conversion), and IMMEDIATE types (a half-timer's omsorgsdag is also 1 day). Byte-identical for full-time 5-day employees (`7.4 ÷ 7.4`). Consumption, payroll norm-credit, and the Skema diff row become mutually consistent. No `× (5 ÷ N)` factor — refuted by the research; no `work_days_per_week` field is added.
*ADR-024 D3 classification record:* date 2026-06-07; from = flat-`÷7.4` day-equivalents with hand-typed hours (internally contradictory for part-timers); to = norm-based day-equivalents; source = Ferieloven §5 stk.1 + §6 stk.2 (LBK 230/2021) + Ferievejledning Dec-2019 Example 3.5/§8.1/§8.4 (verified verbatim); was_agreed = NO; action = `bug-fix-without-recompute` (pre-launch free-correction window); classifier = product owner. The `correct-as-bug` config-surface endpoint machinery is N/A — code convention, not a config-table row.

**D2 — Valuation authority: the per-absence computed feriedage is the authoritative consumption record.** `AbsenceRegistered` gains a `Feriedage` payload field (computed at booking via D1, rounded 4dp **per absence row**); `absences_projection` gains a `feriedage` column (per-absence rows keyed by event_id). ALL read surfaces (year-overview `afholdt`/`saldo`) sum recorded values — no re-derivation, no recompute drift, replay-deterministic by construction. `transferable` + tiles continue to ride the persisted `entitlement_balances` counters, which the booking path increments by the same recorded value — one valuation, two aggregates. **The single-valuation identity is a named invariant, pinned end-to-end by ONE test:** for a booked batch, `atomic-guard delta == Σ AbsenceRegistered.Feriedage == Σ absences_projection.feriedage == used-delta` (the **authoritative** legs; the pre-lock provisional `requestedDays` is excluded by design — it may lag a racing profile change, which is benign because the in-lock guard re-enforces. The pin observes event/projection/used; the guard-delta leg is transitively pinned because `used` increments by exactly the guard delta — Step-7a W2 wording correction). **Mechanically — the explicit two-phase shape (cycle-4 pin):** (pre-lock) the rule-engine advisory validation runs against **provisionally**-computed per-row feriedage; (in-lock) the **authoritative** per-row 4dp feriedage are **re-derived once inside the locked tx** by `ConsumptionCalculator`, and those values are the guard delta, the event payload, and the projection write — every aggregate is a sum of those per-row values; the guard delta must NOT re-divide batched hours (the current `:1014/:1027` sum-hours-then-divide shape is replaced). One rounding site, no aggregate-vs-per-row drift (multi-fractional-absence case pinned). **The identity binds the authoritative in-lock values** (`in-lock guard delta == Σ event Feriedage == Σ projection feriedage == used-delta`); the pre-lock advisory `requestedDays` may differ if a profile change raced — **benign by construction**: `CheckAndAdjustAsync` re-enforces the quota under the locked snapshot and rolls back any breach (the guard, not the advisory rule call, is the enforcement point — the existing `:910-920` TOCTOU contract). **Event OK stamping:** the Skema save path currently stamps `AbsenceRegistered.OkVersion = user.OkVersion` (live; `SkemaEndpoints.cs:941/:1000`) while the valuation resolves OK per absence date — with the event now the authoritative record, the stamp becomes entry-date-resolved (`OkVersionResolver.ResolveVersion(absence.Date)`, the TimeEndpoints TASK-1801 precedent), P4-aligned, OK24/OK26 boundary-tested. **Replay contract:** `ProjectionBackfillService` (+ `tools/ProjectionBackfill`) materializes `feriedage` from `AbsenceRegistered` payloads (pre-S66 null-payload events → `hours/7.4`, the convention in force when written) and applies `EntitlementBalanceRevalued` replacement sets (D4) in event order; a projection-parity test pins rebuilt == live. The live config-row resolution inside `fullDayHours` affects only the **at-booking** computation — historical consumption never silently revalues on config edits.

**D3 — Per-day guard becomes norm-based, with explicit type scoping.**
- Days with `fullDayHours > 0`: the per-day TOTAL across all absence types (today's grouping preserved) is capped at `fullDayHours(e, d)` instead of flat 7.4 (so `dayEquivalent ≤ 1`). The all-types cap is the day-convention choice; narrowing to entitlement-consuming types was considered and rejected (a part-timer's recorded sick day exceeding their norm is the same defect family on the norm-credit side).
- Zero-norm days (weekends): entitlement-consuming rows ⇒ 422 (today they silently consume); non-entitlement rows (SICK_DAY, …) keep today's behavior under the legacy flat-7.4 cap. Mixed-type weekend day: the 422 names the offending entitlement rows only.
- No-profile/pre-employment null ⇒ the existing anchor-422 family (ADR-031 D4 rationale unchanged).
- ANNUAL_ACTIVITY null norm ⇒ fallback basis `fullDayHours = 7.4 × fraction` (the `AbsenceRule` convention) so academics can still take vacation — NOT a blanket null-reject.
- FE prefill: `SkemaGrid` absence-type rows are permanent grid rows, so the prefill trigger is **first focus/edit of an EMPTY absence cell** (not "type selection"); existing values and zero/null-norm days are never overwritten (pinned). Partial days stay legal; for særlige feriedage, fractional taking is explicitly source-sanctioned ("brøkdele af dage"). The flat-7.4 request default dies with the POST contract (D5).

**D4 — Revaluation on profile change.** Profile changes are effectiveFrom=today; absences already booked for dates ≥ effectiveFrom were valued under the old profile. **Trigger: ANY fullDayHours-affecting profile field change — `part_time_fraction` OR `position`** (position drives the ADR-017 D3 override chain, which can alter `WeeklyNormHours`; a fraction-only trigger would silently skip position-driven revaluations). Inside the profile PUT tx: recompute affected absences' feriedage **from the in-hand new values** (the dated resolver owns a separate connection and cannot see the uncommitted row); update the per-absence `absences_projection.feriedage` values AND `entitlement_balances.used` via a **dedicated repo method NOT gated by the booking-path quota-WHERE** (revaluation may push `used` past the cap; implemented as a `(conn,tx)` overload mirroring the existing `AdjustUsedAsync` ungated upsert — not new counter logic) — the projection-row update is **all-or-nothing** (replacement-set row-count mismatch ⇒ rollback, never partial success); emit **`EntitlementBalanceRevalued`** — payload carries the per-absence replacement set `(absence eventId → new feriedage)` + the aggregate used-delta + entitlement type/year (**one event per (type, year) group**) + the triggering profile-change reference — on the consolidated **`employee-{id}`** stream (balance-event lineage, ADR-018 D6), registered in `EventSerializer.EventTypeMap`, **with an ADR-026 `IAuditProjectionMapper` + registration + audit-projection-catalog row**, and an audit row, all in the one tx (ADR-018 D3). **Concurrency contract (pinned):** the profile-PUT revaluation tx and the Skema-save consumption tx both take `pg_advisory_xact_lock(hashtext('employee-' || employeeId))` via ONE shared helper, **acquired FIRST in each critical section, held to commit** (xact-scoped). The Skema path's **consumption-valuation reads (dated profile, agreement/entitlement config, balance) and all consumption writes move inside the locked span** (today they run before the tx opens, `SkemaEndpoints.cs:662` vs `:923`); the **rule-engine HTTP validation call stays OUTSIDE the locked span** (never hold the lock across external-service latency — the rule call is advisory validation; the lock protects the valuation-and-write section only). Note the dated resolver opens its OWN connection — snapshot isolation does NOT serialize these paths; the advisory lock alone does, which is why both critical sections must sit fully inside their lock spans (the PUT commits its profile row before its xact lock releases). Lock ordering: advisory lock strictly precedes any row `FOR UPDATE` (only the profile PUT takes both; the save tx takes no row locks — no AB/BA deadlock). Race-tested. Only `used` is maintained (`planned` has no production writer). Negative remaining is **unclamped and visible** on read surfaces (the negative number renders as-is) — never a silent clamp, never a 500, never blocks the profile change. *(Step-7a W1 correction: a dedicated warning AFFORDANCE — styled banner/flag on the balance surfaces — did not ship in S66 and is a recorded ROADMAP follow-up; the original "surfaces as a warning" wording overstated what was tasked.)*

**D5 — The legacy absence-write bypass is closed; the GET stays.** `POST /api/absences` + the router-orphaned `AbsenceRegistration.tsx`/`WeeklyView.tsx`/`useAbsences.ts` are retired. **`GET /api/absences/{employeeId}` is RETAINED, response-compatible** — Step-0b found a cross-process caller the census missed (`WeeklyCalculationPipeline.cs:73`, Orchestrator service). POST's production FE callers are orphaned, but three regression suites touch the POST surface. Disposition (cycle-3-corrected, per-scenario): the two **HTTP** suites (`PublisherStallReadYourWriteTests` read-your-write, `ProjectionParityTests` parity) are **migrated to `POST /api/skema/{employeeId}/save`** (the surviving absence-write path — preserves `absences_projection` coverage) **with full seeded fixtures** (user + dated profile + agreement code + entitlement config + a guard-passing weekday booking — the bypass worked precisely because it skipped all validation, so a route swap alone cannot); the **forced-rollback** test (`TimeProjectionAtomicTests.RegisterAbsence_OutboxFails_RollsBack`) is **repo-direct (no HTTP), route-agnostic — it survives the POST deletion untouched** (the Skema-path rollback shape is already covered by `SkemaProjectionAtomicTests`). All before the deletion lands; then a deny test pins the invariant: **no absence-write path bypasses the consumption guard.** `RegisterAbsenceRequest` (and its flat-7.4 default) dies with the POST — the Skema path uses its own contract.

**D6 — The §6 stk.2 adjudication + the ADR-031 D6 launch gate.** The "5÷N feriedage per day off" mechanism that ADR-031 D6 scoped this ADR around **does not exist in the state-sector authority** (research doc; 2 adversarial verifications; LBK + Ferievejledning verbatim). §6 stk.2 is week-mirroring; under the shape-free norm model the folding is self-enforcing for week blocks (any worker's week off absents 5 norm-days = 5 feriedage), and D1 is the authority's own per-day mechanism. **The launch gate is therefore satisfied by D1+D2+D3 landing**: the feared over-entitlement of <5-day workers cannot arise because (i) schedule shape remains unrepresentable by design, (ii) week-offs are self-correct, (iii) D1 makes every booked day cost its norm. **Documented residual divergence:** a single enkeltdag taken by a <5-day *compressed-schedule* worker costs 1.0 in-model vs 1.25 under the private-sector practitioner reading — unprescribed by the state authority, unrepresentable without shape modeling; subsumed under the D7 shape limitation. If schedule-shape modeling ever lands, consumption semantics for off-day/single-day cases MUST be revisited in that ADR (binding forward-pointer).

**D7 — Scope fences + accepted limitations.**
- **Schedule shape and real compressed-schedule day-hours stay unmodeled.** `DailyNormCalculator` keeps spreading the weekly norm over 5 weekdays; a full-time 4-day worker's system full-day remains 7.4 (their real 9.25h day is not representable); which weekdays a <5-day worker actually works is unknown — a "vacation" booked on their actual off-day is indistinguishable and consumes. Separate future feature (carries the D6 forward-pointer).
- **Weekday public holidays consume.** The norm path has no holiday awareness (`DanishPublicHolidays` is used only in `SupplementRule`/`ApprovalEndpoints`); vacation booked on e.g. Grundlovsdag resolves a positive norm and consumes. Pre-existing blind spot, documented not silently inherited; future holiday-calendar norm feature.
- **SPECIAL_HOLIDAY**: flat per the verified source (Example 3.5 + §8.4 — no special rules); this ruling does NOT touch the calendar-year accrual simplification (S65 follow-up iii / ADR-030 D9).
- **Payroll untouched**: absence hours flow to payroll as hours (census-verified: no day-count surface anywhere in the payroll chain); ADR-030 D7 §8/§7 settlement stays deferred. ROADMAP follow-ups (iv) fail-loud-vs-graceful and (v) business-timezone stay out of scope; new reads inherit today's conventions.
- **N is a non-concept Backend-side and rule-side**: no rule-engine signature changes; `EntitlementValidationRule` receives converted `requestedDays` as before.
- Precision: per-absence feriedage rounded at 4dp (deterministic); display 2dp.

## Consequences

- The ADR-031 D6 launch gate clears on D1–D3 landing — with the gate's premise corrected on the record rather than silently dropped.
- Part-timer behavior visibly changes (full day 3.7h ⇒ 1.0 feriedag, was 0.5; a 7.4h entry ⇒ 422); full-time 5-day employees are byte-identical. Tests seeding 7.4-for-part-timers are rewritten (Step-0 census).
- Consumption gains a single authoritative record (per-absence recorded feriedage); rebuilds are parity-pinned; one less unguarded write path.
- No new schema field on `employee_profiles`; no HR admin surface change beyond the existing editor; the FE gains the norm prefill.
- Open after S66: schedule-shape modeling (with the D6 consumption forward-pointer); holiday-aware norms; SPECIAL_HOLIDAY calendar-year model; ADR-030 D7 payroll settlement; ROADMAP (iv)/(v).
