## Context

S60 (ADR-030) activated monthly vacation accrual; S61/S62 consolidated `EarnedToDate` into `AccrualMath` and made the accrual **piecewise per-month fraction** (ADR-030 D8: `earned = annualQuota × Σ fraction_m / 12`), so a part-time employee's accrued day-count scaled with `part_time_fraction` (a 50% employee accrued 12.5 days). The 2026-06-03 deep-research established this is **wrong** under Danish Ferieloven: every employee accrues a flat **2,08 dage/md = 25 dage/år regardless of part-time** ("uanset om du arbejder på fuld tid, nedsat tid, eller arbejder få dage om ugen", borger.dk; Ferieloven §5 stk.1). Part-time affects only **feriebetaling** (monetary value, which this system does not compute) and **consumption** (§6 stk.2: a <5-day/week worker's day off consumes >1 feriedag) — never the accrued day-count. The state sector does not diverge (circular 021-24 §3 follows Ferieloven). The encoded model equals the exact "common misconception" the sources flag.

## Decision

**D1 — Vacation day-count is part-time-fraction-independent.** VACATION (25) and SPECIAL_HOLIDAY / særlige feriedage (5) accrue a flat day-count for every employee; `part_time_fraction` does NOT scale the accrued or annual day-count. (Legal basis: Ferieloven §5 stk.1; §6 stk.2 locates part-time pro-ration at consumption; circular 021-24 §3.)

**D2 — Implementation.** `ProRateByPartTime → false` for VACATION + SPECIAL_HOLIDAY (`DefaultEntitlementConfigs` + init.sql seed) ⇒ `effectiveQuota = AnnualQuota` (flat) and the RuleEngine 20%-warning threshold flat. The earned accrual becomes `annualQuota × monthsElapsed / 12` — i.e. the existing `AccrualMath.EarnedToDate(annualQuota, partTimeFraction = 1.0m, ferieaarStart, employmentStart, asOf)`. **Mid-ferieår-hire pro-ration by months-elapsed (ADR-030 D6) is PRESERVED** — a mid-year hire genuinely earns fewer days (fewer months), which is independent of part-time fraction; only the part-time *fraction* drops out of the day-count.

**D3 — Supersedes ADR-030 D8; the S62 piecewise-fraction surface is removed as dead code.** With the accrual fraction-independent, S62's net-new `AccrualMath.EarnedToDatePiecewise`, `FractionPeriod`, and `IEmploymentProfileResolver.GetFractionHistoryAsync` have no correct vacation use and no other consumer (§6/S64 needs `work_days_per_week`, not fraction history) → they are removed. The S61 `AccrualMath` consolidation + the `/summary` and `/series` endpoints STAY (they call the flat `EarnedToDate`). The S62 tests that assert the fraction-scaled curve/caps are rewritten to flat (fraction-independent): `SkemaPiecewiseAccrualTests` is replaced by the renamed **`SkemaAccrualCapTests` ×3** (fail-closed anchor-422 + the flat-cap allow-25/reject-26 bracket — a strict superset of the 2 originally enumerated) and `BalanceSeriesTests.Series_MidFerieaarPartTimeChange_…` becomes the flat unbent-curve test (+ the reconciliation test renamed to fraction-independence, + a new half-time `/summary` == full-time test); the `AccrualMath` unit tests for the removed method are deleted; the past-asOf/monotonic/reconciliation series tests survive.

**D4 — The anchor profile-missing 422 is retained (deliberately); the empty-history 422 is removed with its fetch.** `fractionMatters = isMonthlyAccrual || ProRateByPartTime` stays true via `isMonthlyAccrual`, so the **anchor profile-missing 422** (driven by `GetByEmployeeIdAtAsync` under `fractionMatters`) does NOT relax — its surviving rationale is the **accrual window** (employmentStart + months-elapsed, D6), not the part-time fraction. The S62 **empty-history 422** (`if (fractionHistory.Count == 0)`) was belt-and-suspenders on the now-removed `GetFractionHistoryAsync` fetch; it is **removed along with that fetch** (D3), and the anchor profile-missing 422 covers the missing-profile case. So: anchor 422 stays, empty-history 422 goes — not a relaxation of protection (the anchor guard is the load-bearing one). A blind relaxation of the anchor guard would be the regression.

**D5 — Correction mechanics.** Classified bug-with-no-past-impact (ADR-024 D3) → bug-fix-without-recompute; pre-launch, no replay cost. Seed change applied via an **idempotent UPDATE** block (the `entitlement_configs` seed uses `ON CONFLICT … DO NOTHING`, so a fresh reseed AND an UPDATE are needed for the legacy-upgrade path — the S60 MONTHLY_ACCRUAL precedent). `danish-agreements.md` corrected (the Pro-Rate-Part-Time "Yes" rows for VACATION/SPECIAL_HOLIDAY → "No (day-count); consumption per §6 stk.2").

**D6 — §6 stk.2 consumption is OUT of scope here → deferred to ADR-032 / S64, LAUNCH-BLOCKING.** The legally-correct consumption conversion (a worker on `N` days/week consumes `5 ÷ N` feriedage per day off) needs a new **days-worked-per-week** input the system lacks (`part_time_fraction` is a single scalar that cannot encode schedule shape). **Interim-coupling hazard (documented):** today's pro-rating (the bug D1/D2 removes) partially compensates for the missing §6 conversion, so this fix alone **latently over-entitles fewer-days-per-week workers** (flat 25 days × 1-feriedag/day → >5 weeks). This is SAFE pre-launch because the system cannot even represent a <5-day worker until S64 adds `work_days_per_week`; it becomes unsafe once such employees exist. Therefore **S64 (§6 consumption) is bound as a launch-blocker** — the system must not go live with the flat day-count but no §6 conversion once <5-day schedules are representable.

## Consequences

- A part-time employee now sees the SAME flat 25/5 vacation day-count + the same monthly accrual curve as a full-timer (the `/series` part-time "bend" from S62 is gone — the now-correct behavior). Full-time employees are unchanged.
- S62's piecewise-fraction accrual (ADR-030 D8) is reverted; its net-new code is removed. The S61 consolidation, `/summary`, and `/series` endpoints persist on the flat `EarnedToDate`.
- The `transferable`/Oversigt dashboard (parked) resumes atop the corrected model — its figure is now correct for full-time + 5-day part-timers; the <5-day case lands with S64.
- Open: §6 stk.2 consumption + `work_days_per_week` (ADR-032 / S64, launch-blocking); ADR-030 D7 §8/§7 payroll settlement remains separately deferred.
