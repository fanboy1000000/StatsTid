## Pinned rules (Step-0b cycle-1 absorptions — cycle-2 verification pending)

**R1 — Sequence allocation: odd settlement rows, even reversal lines (the ADR-033 D5 inherited obligation, SETTLED).** Settlement generation `g` (1st, 2nd, …) uses **settlement-row sequence `2g−1`** (original=1, superseding=3, next=5 …); the compensating reversal lines for generation `g` use **export sequence `2g`** (reversal-of-original=2, reversal-of-successor=4 …). No two real lines ever share `(identity, sequence, bucket)`; nothing dedupes away; deterministic and replay-stable. **Generation-from-history (cycle-2 both lenses):** a new settlement of a tuple ALWAYS allocates `g = (highest existing generation for the tuple) + 1` derived from the recorded row sequences — never restarts at 1 — so a future post-bare-reversal revival's arithmetic is already settled (row seq 3 after a bare-reversed gen-1) even though the revival OPERATION itself is out of 3b scope (R3).
